# #05 Advisory Board Decision Analysis

**Use case:** 5 agents debate a high-stakes business decision from different angles, then converge on a recommendation.

---

Create an agent team to analyze a complex business decision.

**The question:** "Should we launch a $7,500 live 6-week AI Leadership Bootcamp for executives and CEOs who want to manage AI teams and integrate AI into their operations — or should we start with a $2,000 self-paced course first to validate demand?"

**Context:** We run Prompt Advisers (https://www.promptadvisers.com/), an AI consulting and education company. We already have a free Skool community with 800+ members. Our audience is a mix of solopreneurs, agency owners, and mid-level managers — but we want to go upmarket to C-suite executives. The bootcamp would cover: building an AI strategy, managing AI-augmented teams, evaluating AI tools for enterprise, and measuring ROI on AI initiatives.

Spawn 5 teammates to investigate different angles. Have them talk to each other to challenge each other's assumptions, like an advisory board debate.

1. **Market Researcher** — Analyze the executive AI education market: who's selling high-ticket AI programs to C-suite, what they charge, what formats work, typical completion rates, and what executives actually want to learn. Quantify the total addressable market. Save to outputs/agent_teams_demo/big_problem/market_research.md.

2. **Audience Gap Analyst** — Investigate the gap between our current audience (solopreneurs, agency owners) and the target audience (executives, CEOs). What marketing channels reach C-suite? What credibility signals do they need before spending $7,500? How different is the sales cycle? Save to outputs/agent_teams_demo/big_problem/audience_analysis.md.

3. **Financial Modeler** — Build a rough financial model comparing both scenarios: Scenario A ($7,500 bootcamp, 15 seats per cohort, live delivery) vs Scenario B ($2,000 self-paced, unlimited seats, async). For each: estimated build costs, revenue per cohort, profit margin, break-even point, time to first dollar, and scaling trajectory over 12 months. Save to outputs/agent_teams_demo/big_problem/financial_model.md.

4. **Competitive Strategist** — Map who's already selling executive AI education: Maven courses, Wharton/Harvard executive programs, Reforge, consulting firms offering workshops. Their pricing, positioning, cohort sizes, and how they'd likely view us as a new entrant. Identify if there's a positioning gap we can exploit. Save to outputs/agent_teams_demo/big_problem/competitive_strategy.md.

5. **Devil's Advocate** — Your job is to find reasons this could fail. Challenge every other teammate's assumptions. Can a YouTube creator credibly sell to Fortune 500 execs? Is the $7,500 price point realistic without enterprise sales infrastructure? What if executives don't take an online bootcamp seriously? Save to outputs/agent_teams_demo/big_problem/devils_advocate.md.

After each teammate completes their initial analysis, have them share their top finding with the group. The Devil's Advocate should then challenge at least 2 other teammates' conclusions. Let the debate play out.

Once consensus (or informed disagreement) emerges, synthesize into a single executive brief with:
- GO / NO-GO / CONDITIONAL recommendation
- Top 3 reasons for the recommendation
- Top 3 risks regardless of decision
- Suggested next steps

Save to outputs/agent_teams_demo/big_problem/executive_brief.md.
